Booking Cancellation Risk

Analysis of roughly 36,000 hotel bookings shows that about one in three bookings cancel. The commercial challenge is not simply demand volume, but demand quality — especially within long lead-time, online, first-time guest bookings.

CEO takeaway: Revenue risk is concentrated in long lead-time, online, first-time guest demand, while repeat guests and corporate bookings are materially more dependable.

Risk profile

Top business risks

  • Long lead-time bookings create the largest pool of revenue uncertainty and cancellation exposure.
  • Online is the largest major demand source but also the weakest-quality major channel.
  • First-time guests are materially less dependable than repeat guests.
  • Low-intent bookings — such as those with no special requests — are much more likely to cancel.

Management implications

Commercial impact

Booking volume alone is not the right success metric. A significant share of future revenue is exposed because the weakest-quality demand sits in long lead-time online channels.

Operational implication

Forecasting, overbooking, and inventory controls should be calibrated more aggressively around cancellation risk, especially in high-risk windows and channels.

Decision implication

Management should prioritize improving booking quality by shifting toward repeat guests and corporate demand while applying stronger controls to high-risk online demand.

What leadership should focus on: Treat cancellation risk as a revenue-management lever, not just an operational metric. The largest upside comes from improving demand quality and acting earlier on high-risk bookings.
Slide 5

Recommended next steps

Immediate actions

  • Tighten overbooking and forecasting controls for long lead-time online bookings.
  • Track cancellation risk by channel, lead time, and guest type on a weekly basis.
  • Introduce simple business rules to flag high-risk bookings earlier in the booking cycle.

Follow-up actions

  • Shift channel strategy toward higher-quality corporate and repeat-guest demand.
  • Use customer intent signals, such as special requests, in pricing and inventory decisions.
  • Measure improvement through reduced cancellation rates and stronger realized revenue conversion.
