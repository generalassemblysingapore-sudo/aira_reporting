Booking Cancellation Risk

This management review focuses on the biggest drivers of cancellations and the two selected response priorities: tightening controls on long lead-time online bookings, and building simple cancellation-risk rules into pricing and inventory decisions.

Executive takeaway: The strongest commercial upside now comes from acting earlier on high-risk demand and embedding cancellation-risk logic into everyday revenue management decisions.

Slide 3

Biggest drivers of cancellation

Primary drivers

  • Long lead time: the strongest driver by far, with 181+ day bookings canceling at 73.9%.
  • Online channel: online bookings cancel at 36.5%, making the largest channel also the weakest-quality major source.
  • First-time guests: non-repeat bookings are materially less reliable than repeat guests.
  • Low booking intent: bookings with no special requests cancel at much higher rates than those showing stronger intent.

Slide 4

Selected management responses

Recommendation 1

Tighten forecasting and overbooking controls around long lead-time online bookings so the business responds earlier to the highest-risk demand pool.

  • Reassess assumptions for long-window online demand
  • Adjust overbooking logic for high-risk booking windows
  • Monitor exposure by booking horizon and channel

Recommendation 3

Build simple cancellation-risk rules into pricing and inventory decisions so booking quality is reflected in commercial controls, not just reporting.

  • Tag high-risk bookings earlier in the cycle
  • Use risk rules in rate and inventory decisions
  • Bring cancellation logic into weekly revenue review
Leadership focus: These two moves matter because they directly address the highest-risk booking population and turn cancellation analysis into commercial action.
Slide 5

Review questions for management

Key review points

  • Are we comfortable with current overbooking controls for long lead-time online demand?
  • What level of cancellation-risk logic should be embedded into pricing and inventory decisions?
  • How quickly can the business operationalize a simple monitoring view for high-risk bookings?

Expected outcome

  • Lower revenue leakage from high-risk booking windows
  • Improved booking quality mix over time
  • Better conversion from booked revenue to realized stay revenue
